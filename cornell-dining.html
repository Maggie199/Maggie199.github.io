UX Designer

troubleshooting

Numerous dining selections

The 745 acre Cornell campus hosts more than 30 eateries, which include All You Care To Eat dining rooms, cafes, coffee houses, and food courts, and convenience stores.

Unavoidable changes

The operating time and menus of different eateries are varied and subjected to changes, causing inconvenience for students to choose dining places and food.

Meet individual needs

With so many options, students need to narrow it down based on their cuisine preferences, health goals, expected price ranges and more, especially for those who are new.

Localized features

When life gets busy, finding out the closest eatery quickly and learning about the expected walking distance & waiting time become extremely important.

Reinvent the food search experience for Cornell students

1

Contextual inquiry & analysis I

Conducted interviews on 8 participants to learn about their daily activities and how they make food decisions. For the purpose of our study, any full time Cornell student was considered an eligible inquiry participant and a potential app user.

Analyzed the responses and recognized common patterns that lead to food decisions, including but not limited to: close to location, price, taste, waiting time and availability, etc...

  • 8 initial interviews
2

Contextual inquiry II

Refined interview questions based on the result in step 1 and conducted a second round interview to understand how important students consider one factor over another and their expectations on our app. Performed quantitative analysis on the interview results.

Designed an online survey to increase our sample size and lear about how do factors like class and campus location affect students' preferences.

  • 20 revised interviews
  • 13 response from online survey
  • quantitative analysis
3

Contextual Analysis II

Created Flow Model based on the result of step2 to identify work roles inside our systemâ€™s work domain and communications among different work roles and system components.

Constructed the Work Activity Affinity Diagram(WAAD) to consolidate and interpret work activity data, which was divided into 3 broad groups: Dining Place Information, Food Information and Special Features.

  • initial and revised flow models
  • work activity affinity diagram
4

Design-Informing Models

Interpreted the information collected in step 3 with 30 Behavior Variables grouped in 3 sets: background, decision process and web-based needs, with Adobe Illustrator. The goal is to identify clusters of participants developing at different points along the sliders.

  • 30 Behavior Variables
5

Persona Design

Analyzed the grouping of the behaviours across on the sliders in step4, and found trends where the same participants are grouped together across multiple variables. Used Adobe Illustrator to create 3 prioritized personas and scenarioes describing how they would interact with our system, also identified their end goals, life goals and experience goals.

  • Primary, Secondary and Supplementary Personas
6

Prototype Design and Usability Testing

Designed the low-fi prototype using Balsamiq based on the goals of personas and the priority of them. According to the scenario we created, personas rarely use mobile devices to purchase/make purchase decision since it's a big deal in terms of money, so that responsive design was not included.

Improved the prototype iteratively by doing Usability Testing with internal stakeholders.

  • Low-fi Prototype (clickable)
  • 4 usability testings
