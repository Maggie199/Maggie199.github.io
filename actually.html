Design Challenges

Automated emergency signal from cars
Works when driver is unconscious
Voice-based message
Reduce the potential for false positives
1

Ideation

Conducted research on reported car accidents over the past two decades and ways to reduce the number of vehicular fatalities. Also evaluated the positioning of SARA in comparison to other emergency systems.

Designed the workflow of the system and identified design objectives:

  • - voice message with exact text that quantifies a legitimate 911 phone call
  • - a “cancel” button to terminate SARA’s automated response and reduce the false positives that inherent with any automated response system
2

Initial design

Came up with an initial design for the cancel button based on features of already existent emergency services and our experiences of driving a motor vehicle.

Initial Design specifications:

  • - Medicine cap, 1-1.5” in diameter
  • - Located underneath the lip of the seat, just below where the horizontal seat adjuster would be
  • - Glowing yellow
  • - Activate by Push-turn
3

Contextual Inquiry

Conducted contextual interviews on 11 drivers, varied by age, gender, driving experience, types of cars, and number of accidents, in order to solicit opinions and obtain feedback on the initial design.

Performed Observations on two 911 operators for approximately half an hour and then conducted an interview, inquiring about their experiences as an operator, their reactions to calls received, and their criteria for determining the legitimacy of calls. Also described our initial design and solicited their comments, feedback and concerns.

4

Contextual Analysis

Conducted qualitative and quantitative analysis on the result from step3 and summarized the following key insights:

  • - Drivers like the idea of automated distress system
  • - Drivers' height prevents a button being placed on the floor
  • - Red is associated with emergencies and is preferred for our system
  • - Drivers prefer a simple push instead of Push-turn
  • - A button that somehow tests cognitive ability is suggested
  • - The three most popular locations were: left-hand side, mirror, and dashboard
  • - Feedback that the button press has been successful is important
  • - Primary info for 911 operators are: type of emergency, where, and a call-back number
  • - Secondary info for 911 operators: number of people, severity of injuries, etc.
  • - 911 operators reported that a male computerized voice is best
  • - 911 operators suggested a delay of 10 seconds between incident and SARA call to reduce false positives
5

Result

Revised our initial design based on the findings listed in step4. The button will remain unlit during normal operation. Should an accident occur, it will glow red, and SARA will ask the driver whether or not they wish to cancel a 911 call. If the driver wishes to, they will press the cancel button once. Afterwards, SARA will prompt them to enter a preset code into their radio dashboard. Upon successful cancellation, SARA will report that the call has been cancelled. However, if this is not successfully completed within 10 seconds, SARA will call 911 with the text, “This is SARA reporting a vehicle accident at [location], call-back may be possible at [driver’s number]. [Additional information as available, including GPS coordinates]…”.
