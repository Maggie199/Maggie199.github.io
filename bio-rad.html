Success Business

Bio-Rad Laboratories(NYSE: BIO and BIOb), ranks in the world's top five life science companies and maintains a solid reputation for quality and innovation.

Growing products portfolio

As Bio-Rad gets more products in its portfolio, it seeks to sell more commoditized products via web, so that sales representatives could focus on higher value products.

10+ year old website

The bio-rad.com was launched back in 1997, and has lots of room for improvement to be a trendy website: hero images, card layouts, responsive design, etc...

Low Findability and Discoverability

Bio-rad.com uses a Deep hierarchy info architecture; product detail pages are 6 levels down from the homepage, and not reflecting attribute priorities in the Mini-IA

Contextual inquiry

Conducted interviews on a wide range of internal stakeholders, including product managers, sales representatives, technical support and customer service, to learn about the most typical scenario when customers decide they need a thermal cycler, what information they need to make a purchase decision, how they interact with bio-rad to get those info, and most importantly what kind of group is likely to be our online purchasing target users.

Researched on the websites of 6 top competitors in life science field and 6 non-peer vendors to compare their features and content, as well as branstorm the best online purchasing experience.

  • 10 interviews on internal stakeholders
  • 12 competitive usability evaluations
2

Contextual Analysis

Put together the data collected in step 1 in spreadsheets and performed data interpretation using customer journey map, which illustrates the steps that customers go through in engaging with our online purchasing system.

  • Original customer journey map
  • Revised customer journey map
3

Contextual inquiry II

Conducted interviews on existing customers from UC Berkerley, UC Davis and the Buck Institute, to learn about the most important factors that influence their purchase decisions and their opinion about the current T100 webpage.

Designed an online survey using Survey Monkey upon accomplishment of the Questionnaire Design for Social Surveys course on Coursera, in order to further collect the above information from a larger user group. The survey includes 38 questions in total, and up to 13 questions for a single user deponding on his role(lab manager, post doc, researcher and purchaser). Most questions are very simple, and the number of open-ended question is limited to 1 per user so it could be finished in ~5 mins. This Flow Chart describes the page logic amongst questions.

  • 8 interviews on customers
  • 38 online survey questions
